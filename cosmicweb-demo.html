The Cosmic Web — filaments, voids, and real clusters

The regime one step out from the Local Group: galaxies and clusters strung along filaments that wrap enormous empty voids. The foam is a node-and-filament seeded scatter; the labelled clusters are real, at their true distances. Rendered by figures/cosmicweb.js on the same primitives in figures/figures.js.

Drag to pan, scroll to zoom, or jump to a region. The faint strands are filaments between cluster knots; the dim points are a representative swarm of galaxies that gather along them, leaving the voids dark. The labelled clusters carry real distances from us; the Local Group is marked "you are here" — one knot among many. We sit on the outskirts of the Virgo Supercluster, inside the larger Laniakea supercluster (~520 Mly across), drifting toward the Great Attractor.

A standalone demo, not one of the frozen editions. The honest split: a real catalog — Virgo, Coma, the Great Attractor, and the rest, at real distances — for the known clusters, and a seeded node-and-filament foam, representative and never named, for the web itself. Distances are real; the top-down 2D bearings are a schematic diagram, not a sky map. Laniakea is named as the supercluster we are inside, not a point "over there". Live-only by design — no sealed poster of its own; the composed cosmic figure's JS-off floor stays the Milky Way.
