The Local Group — real galaxies, one seeded swarm

The regime one step out from the galaxy: where the spiral was a statistical star field, the Local Group is catalog-primary — a handful of real named galaxies at their true distances from the Milky Way, plus the anonymous many as a seeded dwarf swarm. Rendered by figures/localgroup.js on the same primitives in figures/figures.js.

Drag to pan, scroll to zoom, or jump to a region. The two bright spirals are the Milky Way (marked "you are here") and Andromeda — the dominant pair of the Group, ~2.5 Mly apart. The labelled members carry real distances; the faint cloud is a representative seeded swarm of the ~70 dwarf satellites, not specific named galaxies. Zoom toward the Milky Way and the next regime out hands back to the galaxy.

A standalone demo, not one of the frozen editions. The honest split: a real catalog — real names, real distances — for the known members, and a seeded swarm, representative and never named, for the anonymous dwarfs. Distances are real; the top-down 2D arrangement is a schematic diagram, not a sky map. This regime is live-only by design — it carries no sealed poster of its own; in the composed cosmic figure the JS-off floor stays the Milky Way.
