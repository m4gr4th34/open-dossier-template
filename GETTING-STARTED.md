# GETTING-STARTED.md — Your first dossier, start to finish

You have an idea — maybe one the gatekeepers won't touch. By the end of
this page you'll have turned it into a live, citable, timestamped
publication that explains itself and checks its own claims, working
alongside an AI agent that does the heavy lifting while **you** supply the
ideas, the intuition, and the judgment.

No prior experience with Git, LaTeX, or web code is needed. If you can
describe your idea in plain English and tell when an answer smells wrong,
you can publish a dossier.

> **The deal.** The AI does the literature search, the drafting, the math
> typesetting, the verification code, and the web pages. You do four
> things only: bring the idea, sanity-check the results, decide what's
> established versus what's an open claim, and press the buttons that
> publish. That's the whole division of labor.

---

## What you'll need (15 minutes, once)

1. **A GitHub account** — free, at github.com. Your publication lives here.
2. **An AI coding agent** — e.g. Claude Code (Pro/Max plan). It reads the
   `AUTHORING.md` and `CLAUDE.md` files in the template automatically and
   already knows the production rules.
3. **A Zenodo account** — free, "log in with GitHub." This mints your
   permanent DOI. (Set up once, in Step 6.)

No local LaTeX, no web tooling — the agent and GitHub's servers do all of
it.

## A complete worked example

We'll follow one real, contested idea the whole way:

> **"What can be inferred about UAP propulsion from the public kinematic
> data — and what would a genuine exotic-propulsion signature have to look
> like?"**

This is the right example precisely *because* it's the kind of question
the traditional stack punishes you for asking. Established science has
documented the anomalies and the secrecy; the exotic-propulsion
conclusion is exactly the kind of bold claim that gets a credentialed
researcher mocked — not refuted, mocked — and therefore unpublished. This
format is built for exactly that situation: it lets you state the bold
claim **and** make it unattackable, by labeling with total precision what
is established, what is restricted, and what is the open challenge. The
honest skeptic is left with nowhere to plant a dismissal. That's the
weapon. Substitute your own contested idea at every step.

---

### Step 1 — Spawn your repository (2 min)

Open your AI agent and say:

> "Go to https://github.com/m4gr4th34/open-dossier-template and
> follow the 'Spawning a new dossier' ritual in its README to
> create a new dossier from it. Name the new repo
> dossier-uap-propulsion. I'm [your name], [your affiliation or
> 'independent researcher, city']. Read AUTHORING.md and CLAUDE.md
> in the template before you start — they contain the production
> rules."

The agent clones the template and does the rename pass. You now own a repo
with all the machinery.

### Step 2 — Map the terrain honestly (the part that feels like research)

A conversation, not a form:

> "Do a prior-art reconnaissance on UAP propulsion inference from public
> data. I want three buckets, each rigorously sourced: what is ESTABLISHED
> (documented sensor records, official acknowledgments, the AARO/NASA/Navy
> material, the kinematic claims with their measurement caveats); what is
> ADJACENT (proposed physical mechanisms, the energy/acceleration
> analyses); and what is genuinely OPEN (the exotic-propulsion question
> nobody has closed either way). Build the claim ledger and label every
> entry by how well it's actually verified — no inflation, no dismissal."

The agent searches and returns a map. **Your job — the real intellectual
work:** read it with everything you know switched on. You've spent years
on this; the agent has breadth but not your depth. Correct it. Tell it
what it's underweighting and what mainstream sources quietly omit. This
back-and-forth is the research, and it's irreplaceably yours.

> **What "honest" buys you here.** Stating flatly, as ESTABLISHED, that
> objects with anomalous observed kinematics are documented and that
> relevant data is demonstrably restricted — with citations — is not
> timid. It is the strongest possible ground, because it is *true and
> sourced*, and no one can knock you off it. Saving "exotic propulsion" as
> the labeled OPEN CHALLENGE is not a retreat — it is the move that makes
> the whole dossier impossible to dismiss and impossible to ignore.

### Step 3 — Draft, with fearless honest labels (the agent leads)

> "Draft the manuscript from paper/manuscript-template.tex per
> AUTHORING.md. State the established anomalies and the data restriction
> plainly and boldly — these are documented facts, not speculation. Label
> the exotic-propulsion inference OPEN-UNVERIFIED and frame it as an open
> challenge to the field, with the specific signature that would settle
> it. I want the bold claim on the page — correctly labeled, so it's
> armor, not ammunition for critics."

The agent writes the formal paper and fills the claim ledger. **Your job:**
make sure every label is true — in *both* directions. Don't let it
soft-pedal a documented anomaly into "alleged," and don't let it assert
the exotic conclusion as settled. Truth in labeling cuts both ways, and
that symmetry is your credibility.

### Step 4 — Let it attack its own work (automatic)

> "Run the verification script and citation audit. Then do an adversarial
> red-team pass as the most hostile skeptical referee you can simulate —
> the kind who wants to laugh this off — and publish every finding,
> including the ones I won't like."

Every number recomputes; every citation is checked; the skeptic's best
shots get written down *in the paper* and answered. **Your job:** the
reality check, and a specific discipline here — a claim the red-team
can't break is worth ten exclamation points. Let the adversarial pass
make your strong claims strong and your open claims honest.

### Step 5 — Build the readable editions (the agent leads)

> "Build all three web surfaces per AUTHORING.md: the interactive edition
> with the verification console, the self-explaining edition with tappable
> terms and citation summaries so a curious outsider needs no
> prerequisites, and the audit trail."

Now your contested idea is a slick, self-explaining publication that a
skeptical scientist can read end to end without a single eye-roll —
because every claim is exactly as strong as its evidence, no more, no
less. **Your job:** read it as the toughest fair-minded outsider you can
imagine. Where would they balk? Tell the agent; tighten it.

### Step 6 — Publish (you press the buttons)

> "Walk me through DEPLOY.md."

The agent guides you: turn on the website, connect Zenodo once, tag the
first release. The instant you publish, your DOI is minted at CERN, your
release is anchored in the Bitcoin blockchain, and your site goes live.

**You are now published** — versioned, citable, timestamped, self-checking.
No journal, no gatekeeper, no permission, no fee. Your priority on the
idea is now cryptographically yours, on a date no one can forge or erase.

### Step 7 — Let the world in

Share the link. Skeptics and allies file GitHub issues against specific
claims; you answer in public; corrections become dated amendments
absorbed into the living document. Your OPEN-CHALLENGE claims are open
invitations — the first person to settle one, for or against, is credited
in your next version. If you're right, the timestamped record will show
you said it early, clearly, and with the receipts. The paper is alive: it
improves in the open, and every version is preserved forever.

## Why this is worth trying — especially for the bold

- **It protects the heretic.** The format's one rule — *every label is
  true* — is exactly the armor a researcher with an unpopular-but-correct
  idea needs. State the bold claim; label it honestly; become
  undismissable.
- **You find out fast, and you can't fool yourself.** Prior-art recon in
  an hour; a public red-team and a self-checking script that keep your own
  enthusiasm honest. Conviction and demonstration live in different
  columns of the ledger — and keeping them straight is what makes you
  credible to the people not yet convinced.
- **It's yours the moment you publish.** Timestamped priority, no review
  queue, no gatekeeper deciding whether you're credentialed enough to have
  the idea. The data may vindicate you next year or next decade; the
  record will show you were there first, and rigorous.
- **History rewards the ones who showed their work.** The right side of an
  empirical question is the side that turns out correct *and* documented.
  Publish the bold claim with the cleanest labels in the field, and when
  the evidence lands, your dossier reads as foresight, not luck.
- **The cost of trying is an afternoon.** Worst case, your idea meets its
  reality checks and you learn something real. Best case, you've planted a
  flag nobody can pull up.
